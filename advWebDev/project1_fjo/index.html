Tips

  • Go Mobile First - Approximately 1 in every 7 people on earth use their mobile devices to access the internet
  • Get Acquainted with Media Queries - Media queries check for a device's resolution, height, width and orientation
  • Understand What Mobile Means for Your Users - Use analytics to figure out why a user is visiting your website on their phone
  • Use Percentages - Instead of designing breakpoints for every possible viewport, you set a maximum layout size
  • The Need for Speed - Smartphone users expect a site to load in under 4 seconds. Main reason behind a slow site are non-optimized images
  • Eliminate the Unnecessary - Get rid of excessive elements, not only for your user experience, but also for website's speed
  • To Hamburger or Not to Hamburger - Keep less popular links in the hamburger menu navigation
  • Make it Readable - Don't make your users squint to read or pinch-to-zoom. Make your text size large enough to read from a smaller screen
  • Use the Right Button Size - Avoid small button sizes. Padding increases the tappable area, but margins do not
  • Design for Screen Orientation - Landscape orientation wins over portrait orientation 59% to 41%
